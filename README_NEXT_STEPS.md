This starter pack is tailored to Moustafa Abdelwanis and the Academic Pages template.

What to do first:

1. Replace `your-github-username` in `_config.yml` with your real GitHub username.
2. Put your headshot at `images/profile.png`.
3. Upload your CV to `files/Moustafa_Abdelwanis_CV.pdf`.
4. Copy `_data/navigation.yml` into your repository.
5. Copy the pages in `_pages/` into your repository.
6. Copy the publication files in `_publications/` into your repository.
7. Fill in any additional links you want to show publicly, such as LinkedIn, ORCID, Scopus, ResearchGate, or GitHub.

Strong recommendations:

- Do not publish your phone number on the website.
- Do not publish referee names and emails on the website.
- Do not leave placeholder links such as demo GitHub, demo PubMed, or demo Bluesky links.
- Do not keep empty navigation tabs.

Suggested structure:

- Home: concise positioning statement + selected publications + recognition
- Research: 3-4 research themes with methods and applications
- Publications: generated from `_publications`
- Teaching: only keep if you will maintain it
- Awards: recognition, conferences, reviewer roles, grants
- CV: direct PDF download

Good next additions:

- a professional photo
- ORCID and LinkedIn
- software or dataset pages
- a short news section for accepted papers, awards, and talks
- exact publication dates in the `_publications` files

If you later want to scale publication entry creation, Academic Pages includes a markdown generator that can convert structured TSV or CSV-like data into publication markdown files.
