I am a Ph.D. candidate in Engineering Systems and Management at Khalifa University in Abu Dhabi. My research sits at the intersection of operations management, AI in healthcare, human-AI interaction, simulation, and optimization. I study how intelligent systems can improve healthcare and service operations while remaining safe, human-centered, and practically deployable.

## Research Themes

### AI in healthcare and human-AI interaction
I study how healthcare professionals adopt AI tools, what barriers affect trust and usability, and how risks such as automation bias can be identified and mitigated.

### Simulation and optimization for service systems
I develop simulation-based optimization methods for multi-server, inventory-constrained, and cross-trained service systems, with applications in healthcare and operations management.

### Data-driven clinical decision support
I apply machine learning and analytics to support clinical assessment and risk prediction, with current interests in cardiac autonomic neuropathy, depression severity assessment, and healthcare analytics.
